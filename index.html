Comparing the Education Level in Florida: General Population vs. Hispanic Population

By: Carina Daidone

The population in the state of Florida has seen a huge shift towards a larger Hispanic population. With this in mind, I took into consideration the education level of the general Florida population compared to the specific Hispanic population. In particular, does the Hispanic population follow the trend of the general population with more females than males holding a high school degree? Do more females than males in the Hispanic population hold a bachelor's degree or higher?

Source: Pew Research Center: "Hispanic population reaches record 55 million, but growth has cooled"

Source: U.S. Census, Florida Education Easy Stats

In general, the population within Florida favors toward more females over males holding any kind of degree. The only category in which the number of males are greater than females is the "less than high school" category.

Source: U.S. Census, Florida Education Easy Stats

The same education level trends found within the general population is continued within the Hispanic population. Also, like the rest of the Florida population, there are more high school degrees awarded than college degrees.
